# Christi Nomen

## Structured

- **Key theme:** Mixed
- **Theme (one sentence):** Leo XIII calls on bishops and the faithful to support the Society for the Propagation of the Faith in order to fund missions and educational works aimed at reunifying the Eastern Churches with Rome.
- **Summary:** The document opens by recalling Leo XIII's long-standing mission to "spread the name and reign of Christ" and to "call back the mutinous and the wandering to the bosom of the Church." It then pivots to what the Pope identifies as a "new need and one more grave": the practical financial and organisational resources required to pursue the union of the Roman and Eastern Catholic Churches. He asks bishops to grow membership and contributions to the Society for the Propagation of the Faith, and links material generosity directly to the spiritual goal of ecclesial unity, framing donations as both personally salutary and ecclesially necessary.
- **Response to:** The document explicitly responds to (a) the prior apostolic letter *Praeclara* (June 1894), which invited all nations to Christian unity, and (b) Leo XIII's earlier letters on safeguarding Eastern Rites and consultations with Eastern patriarchs about reunion. It also acknowledges "hard times" that limit the Holy See's own financial capacity to support missions.
- **Prescriptive towards:** The text looks ahead to the reunion of the Roman and Eastern Catholic Churches ("the union of the Roman and Eastern Catholic Churches"), the opening of schools and seminaries suited to Eastern national character, and the wider distribution of Catholic literature in Eastern regions. It anticipates that increased lay generosity will translate into the concrete infrastructure needed to make union possible.
- **Tension:** The core tension is between the magnitude of the spiritual and ecclesial goal (universal Christian unity, conversion of those who have "seceded") and the inadequacy of current resources—financial, ministerial, and institutional. A secondary tension exists between respecting Eastern rites and national character ("it will fit their national character congenially") and the overarching drive toward Roman-led unity ("one sheepfold and one Pastor").

## Unstructured

*Christi Nomen* is a short, tightly structured fundraising appeal dressed in theological and pastoral language. The argument moves in three steps: (1) Leo recalls his ongoing concern for mission and the modest fruits of earlier appeals; (2) he names the specific new urgency—Eastern reunion—and explains why it exceeds present resources; (3) he makes a direct call to bishops to expand the Society for the Propagation of the Faith.

The document's tone is predominantly **pastoral-directive**: warm in its address to "Venerable Brethren," but firm in its expectations. Leo uses the rhetorical device of acknowledging difficulty ("We do not deny that this goal involves great difficulties") while immediately subordinating that difficulty to divine providence, thereby foreclosing any excuse for inaction.

The framing is markedly pragmatic. Rather than dwelling on doctrinal arguments for reunion, Leo frames the question almost entirely in terms of logistics: schools, seminaries, literature, clergy formation, and the costs these entail. The spiritual dimension—prayer, trust in God, the spread of "Christian wisdom"—functions as the motivation and legitimation for what is essentially a material appeal.

Notable is the explicit linkage between lay financial contribution and papal agency: "With their contributions We can accomplish what We have resolved upon for the good of the Eastern Church." This positions ordinary Catholics as co-actors in a papal project, a mobilising rhetoric that simultaneously flatters and obligates them.

The document also illustrates Leo XIII's broader ecclesial strategy of working through existing institutions (the Society for the Propagation of the Faith, the Society for the Schools of the East) rather than creating new structures, leveraging established networks to pursue new goals.
