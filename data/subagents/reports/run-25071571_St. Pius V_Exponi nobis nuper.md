# Exponi nobis nuper

## Structured

- **Key theme:** Mixed
- **Theme (one sentence):** Pope Pius V confirms and perpetually validates the royal foundation of a university of sacred and human letters in Lima (City of Kings), granting it all the privileges of the University of Salamanca, for the spiritual instruction and Catholic formation of the newly converted indigenous peoples of the Indies.
- **Summary:** This apostolic brief formally confirms the establishment of a studium generale (university) at the House of Santa María del Rosario of the Order of Preachers in Lima, originally erected by Emperor Charles V and now under King Philip II. The document recounts that vast portions of the Indies have been brought to the Christian faith and that the converted population lacks instruction in letters and in evangelical law; the university is therefore founded to remedy this. Pius V grants apostolic authority to the foundation, supplies any defects of form or substance, and equates the Lima university's privileges entirely with those of the University of Salamanca, declaring all contrary attempts null and void.
- **Response to:** The document responds to: (1) a petition (supplicatio) presented on behalf of the university, recounting that the Indies had been reduced to Christian obedience under Charles V and now Philip II; (2) the prior royal foundation/erection of the university by those monarchs, which lacked formal apostolic confirmation; (3) the stated fact that the converted people ("dictum populum") were ignorant of letters ("lipsarum litterarum ignarum") and needed instruction in faith and evangelical law.
- **Prescriptive towards:** (1) The perpetual validity and inviolable observance of the university's foundation, dotation, and privileges by all persons of any rank (episcopal, archiepiscopal, patriarchal, ducal, royal, imperial); (2) the ongoing work of converting and confirming the faith of the indigenous population; (3) future judges and commissioners, who are explicitly stripped of any contrary interpretive authority ("sublata eis… quavis aliter judicandi et interpretandi facultate").
- **Tension:** The document implies a tension between the pastoral/missionary imperative — educating and confirming a vast newly converted population in the faith — and the institutional fragility of a foundation created by royal rather than direct apostolic authority. The brief supplies "defects of solemnity and substance" and preemptively declares any challenge to the document null, suggesting awareness that the legitimacy of the foundation could be contested. There is also an implicit tension between the universalizing mission of the Church and the logistical and spiritual challenge of reaching a populous region far from Rome.

## Unstructured

The document is a formal apostolic brief, dense with legal Latin formulae, written in the highly compressed style of papal chancery documents. Its argument unfolds in three recognizable moves: (1) a narration (expositio) of the historical and pastoral circumstances justifying the action — the Christianisation of the Indies under the Spanish crown, the numerical growth of the converted population, and the pre-existing royal foundation of the university in the City of Kings; (2) the dispositio — the Pope's apostolic confirmation, supplementation of any defects, and re-erection of the university with full Salamanca-equivalent privileges; (3) a clausula irritans — a sweeping declaration that any contrary action, whether knowing or unknowing, is to be considered null, by any authority whatsoever.

The tone is strongly authoritative, not pastoral. There is no exhortation, no meditation, no Scripture citation. The document functions as a legal instrument: its spiritual concern (spreading the faith, instructing the converted) is the stated rationale, but the mechanism is purely juridical — confirmation, supply of defects, grant of privileges, and prohibition of interference.

The key framing device is the equation of Lima with Salamanca. By granting "omnia et singula privilegia" of Salamanca to the Lima university, the document symbolically incorporates the new world institution into the existing European juridical and intellectual order. The university is not simply a mission school; it is — by papal decree — the equivalent of one of Europe's most prestigious universities. This framing elevates the dignity of the converted population's education while simultaneously anchoring it within an existing, tested institutional structure.

Notable is the brief's explicit attention to legal completeness: the Pope not only confirms the foundation but also absolves the recipients from any ecclesiastical censures they may be under ("a quibusvis excommunicationis suspensionis et interdicti… sententiis"), supplies "omissions of solemnities and substantial forms," and reaffirms that the document itself can never be impugned for subreptionis or obreptionis (procedural defects in obtaining a papal rescript). This language signals that the document was drafted with awareness of potential canonical challenges, and the brief is designed to be bulletproof against them.

The missionary-educational purpose — confirming the people in faith ("in fide et lege evangelica confirmandum") and instructing those ignorant of letters — is stated at the outset but thereafter serves mostly as the stated cause (causa finalis) for the legal machinery that follows. The document's primary interest is institutional permanence, not missionary method.
