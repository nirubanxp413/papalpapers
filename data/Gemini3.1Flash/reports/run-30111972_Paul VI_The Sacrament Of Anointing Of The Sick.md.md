## Unstructured

### 1. Nature of the document
*Sacram Unctionem Infirmorum* is an Apostolic Constitution. It is a legislative instrument of the Holy See, functioning as a legal "correction" to the *Codex Iuris Canonici*. Its tone is administrative and authoritative, designed to resolve ambiguity in the application of the sacrament.

### 2. Argument structure
The document utilizes a "restorative" logic: it identifies a historical deviation (the narrowing of the sacrament to the dying), cites the "original" scriptural and early patristic intent (James 5:14), and uses the authority of Vatican II to mandate a return to that broader, more inclusive practice.

### 3. Key conceptual move: From death-preparation to illness-care
The document attempts to decouple the sacrament from the "imminent death" requirement. This is a significant shift in the Church's social engagement with the sick. By broadening the criteria, the Church moves from a "last rites" model (which often signaled the end of social/medical intervention) to a "supportive care" model.

### 4. Practical accommodation as a theological signal
The permission to use other vegetable oils is a rare instance of the Vatican acknowledging that the "matter" of a sacrament is contingent upon global economic and environmental realities. This reflects a pragmatic, if limited, recognition of the Church's globalized, post-colonial reality.

Public discourse at the time was dominated by the Vietnam War, the Cold War, and the aftermath of *Humanae Vitae*. The document was received primarily as an internal administrative update for clergy.
