### Reflective
The encyclical looks back at:
*   **Historical Jansenism and Gnosticism:** Explicitly critiques the historical and contemporary tendency to separate the "spiritual" from the "bodily/affective."
*   **Devotional Tradition:** Synthesizes centuries of Sacred Heart devotion, specifically rehabilitating the 17th-century revelations of Margaret Mary Alacoque and the 19th-century "Little Way" of Thérèse of Lisieux.
*   **Modern "Heartlessness":** Interprets the rise of consumerism and digital isolation as a loss of the "inner center," citing Dostoevsky and Heidegger as diagnostic tools for the modern condition.

*   **Fraternal Reparation:** Shifts the focus of "reparation" from ritual sacrifice to social action and reconciliation, aiming to influence the Church’s social engagement.
*   **Spiritual Integration:** Calls for a "synthesis" of faith and life to prevent the "activism" that Francis views as a failure of modern pastoral ministry.
