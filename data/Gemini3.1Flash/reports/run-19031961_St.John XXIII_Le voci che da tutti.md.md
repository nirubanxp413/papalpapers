### 2. Reflective Analysis
The document is highly reflective, functioning as a "dossier of precedent." By tracing the evolution of Josephine devotion, John XXIII is performing a classic papal maneuver: legitimizing a new, potentially disruptive event (the Council) by embedding it within a century of established, conservative piety. It interprets the "modern world" not through political analysis, but through the lens of a "hidden" saint who provides a model of stability amidst the "thunder" of the 20th century.

### 3. Prospective Analysis
The document prescribes a "quietist" approach to the Council. It attempts to shape the *internal culture* of the Council participants—clergy and laity—by demanding silence and prayer. It claims that this spiritual preparation will lead to "unity and peace." Historically, this proved to be a miscalculation of the Council's actual trajectory, which was characterized by intense, public, and often noisy theological debate rather than the "serene silence" requested by the Pope.
